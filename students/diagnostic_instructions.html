SAT Diagnostic Exam Instructions

Please read all instructions carefully before beginning.

About This Diagnostic

This diagnostic exam is designed to assess your current SAT readiness and identify areas for improvement. The test simulates the actual SAT exam structure and timing, but is shortened for demonstration purposes.

Your results will help your tutor create a personalized study plan targeting your specific strengths and weaknesses.

Exam Structure

This diagnostic exam consists of 4 modules divided into 2 sections: Reading/Writing and Math. Each section has 2 modules with 3 questions per module for demonstration purposes.

Section Module Questions Time
Reading and Writing Module 1 3 questions 8 minutes
Module 2 3 questions 8 minutes
Math Module 1 3 questions 8 minutes
Module 2 3 questions 8 minutes

Total exam time: 32 minutes (excluding breaks)

Note: The actual SAT exam is approximately 2 hours and 14 minutes with 98 total questions.

Important Rules

  • Once you begin a module, the timer will start. You must complete the module within the allocated time.
  • You cannot pause the timer during a module.
  • You can mark questions for review and revisit them within the same module.
  • After completing a module, you cannot return to it.
  • There is a short 1-minute break between modules.
  • You can use the provided digital calculator for the Math sections only.

Tips for Success

  • Read each question carefully before answering.
  • Manage your time wisely. Don't spend too much time on any single question.
  • Use the "Mark for Review" feature to flag questions you want to revisit within the module.
  • For Reading passages, read the questions first to know what to look for in the passage.
  • For Math, use the digital calculator strategically but rely on mental math for simpler calculations.
  • Answer all questions—there is no penalty for wrong answers.

After the Exam

Once you complete all modules, you'll receive a comprehensive score report that breaks down your performance by section and topic. Your tutor will review these results with you to develop a targeted study plan.
